The food we consume contributes to climate change. The production, packaging and transportation of food all consumes energy and results in carbon emissions which threaten to raise average global surface temperatures.

However, not all foods are equal...
The Food Carbon Footprint Tracker (FCFT) provides the opportunity to calculate the resultant carbon dioxide from the food you eat, called your "Food Carbon Footprint".

This website brings awareness in people about rapidly changing global climate, and offers personalised and practical ways to reduce the carbon footprint of your diet, reducing your impact on climate change.

Carbon Footprint

Your carbon footprint is the sum of all emissions of greenhouse gases, which were induced by your activities in a given time frame.

In other words: When you drive a car, the engine burns fuel which creates a certain amount of CO2, depending on its fuel consumption and the driving distance. When you heat your house with oil, gas or coal, then you also generate CO2.

Even if you heat your house with electricity, the generation of the electrical power may also have emitted a certain amount of CO2. When you buy food and goods, the production of the food and goods also emitted some quantities of CO2.

You can explore the site or calculate your Diet Carbon Footprint
